# CO2-EQ Consumption of Grid Electricity: Emission Factors



## Thailand

Thailand's [Greenhouse Gas Management Organization (TGO)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand_Greenhouse_Gas_Management_Organization) announced new electricity emission factors, setting the Scope 2 factor at 0.4750 kgCO₂e/kWh and Scope 3 at 0.0812 kgCO₂e/kWh for 2026. These updated factors, applicable to residential and organizational consumption, are effective from January 1, 2026, and are based on updated fuel data.

**Key Details**

- **Methodology**: Based on IPCC 5th Assessment Report (AR5) metrics, utilizing data from the Ministry of Energy, EGAT, MEA, and PEA.
- **Grace Period**: Previous grid coefficients are allowed for use until March 31, 2026, for historical inventory audits.

### Basis for the updated emission factors

TGO stated that the revised factors were derived from data on fuel extraction, refining, and transportation, alongside fuel combustion and electricity transmission and distribution losses. The updated values were developed using input from:

- The Ministry of Energy
- The Electricity Generating Authority of Thailand (EGAT)
- The Metropolitan Electricity Authority (MEA)
- The Provincial Electricity Authority (PEA)

### Effective date and transition period

The new emission factors will take effect on 1 January. However, organisations may continue using the previous factors until 31 March, allowing a transition period for updating internal carbon accounting systems.

### Example Calculation for 1 kWh of Consumption

If a home or organization consumes **1 kWh** of grid electricity in Thailand, the total greenhouse gas impact is calculated by adding the Scope 2 and Scope 3 components together:

\(\text{Total\ Emissions}=\text{Scope\ 2}+\text{Scope\ 3}\)
\(\text{Total\ Emissions}=0.4750\text{\ kgCO}_{2}\text{e}+0.0812\text{\ kgCO}_{2}\text{e}=\mathbf{0.5562}\text{\ kgCO}_{\mathbf{2}}\text{e}\)

- **Result:** 
  - ==Consuming **1 kWh** of electricity results in **\(0.5562\) kg of carbon dioxide equivalent** emitted into the atmosphere.==
